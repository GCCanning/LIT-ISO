# FreePixel Acquisition Plan

Purpose: collect only useful FreePixel packs through official free download routes, without scraping individual assets or bulk crawling the site.

## Policy

- Use official pack download pages only.
- Do not scrape individual asset image URLs.
- Do not bulk crawl FreePixel pages.
- Preserve original ZIPs and license/readme files.
- Treat assets as reference/oracle material until licensing and art direction are approved.
- Do not use FreePixel assets for AI training unless FreePixel/Sprixen explicitly grants training rights.

## Download Note

The itch.io free pack pages expose the ZIP through the standard itch purchase/download widget. The first verified page lists:

- `free-rpg-animations-pixel-art-270-sprites-assets.zip`
- size: 3.9 MB
- price: free / pay what you want

The direct download link is generated by itch's widget after choosing "No thanks, just take me to the downloads." Codex should not bypass that by scraping individual asset URLs. Manual/interactive download through the official page is the clean path.

## After Download

1. Put ZIP files in:
   `C:\Projects\Pixel Pipeline\sources\freepixel\zips`
2. Extract to:
   `C:\Projects\Pixel Pipeline\sources\freepixel\extracted\<pack-id>`
3. Run inventory:
   file count, dimensions, alpha, sheet detection, direction order detection.
4. Generate contact sheets into:
   `C:\Projects\Pixel Pipeline\generated\freepixel_inventory\<pack-id>`
5. Classify each pack:
   direction oracle, animation reference, tile reference, prop reference, item icon reference, UI reference, rejected.

## Current Verdict

FreePixel is useful for LIT-ISO, but not all packs are isometric. The strongest immediate value is:

- 8D character direction reference
- animation naming and frame-count reference
- isometric blocks/height material reference
- RPG prop/item/UI vocabulary

FreePixel should not replace our original art pipeline. It should give us reliable direction/action references and content taxonomy while our own Sprixen-style generator learns to produce original assets.

### First-Pass Isometric Usefulness

**Most useful for LIT-ISO now**

- RPG Environment: strong prop/interactable reference. Many bottom-anchored objects and some isometric altar/stone/camp pieces.
- RPG Essentials: strong VFX and archetype vocabulary. Useful for VFX generator and high-fantasy class references.
- RPG Characters & Companions: useful character/mount/companion vocabulary and some facing variants. Not packaged as clean animation sheets.
- RPG Tilesets: mixed. Contains some isometric chunks/cliffs/rooms, but also top-down sheets, interiors, walls, and props. Use as reference only until manually filtered.

**Useful, but not specifically isometric**

- RPG Items & Loot
- RPG Weapons & Armor
- RPG UI Kit

These are good icon/UI/equipment vocabulary packs. Isometric perspective is mostly irrelevant for these lanes.

**Weak for the diagonal problem**

- RPG Animations: useful for VFX/action vocabulary, but the downloaded free pack is mostly individual attack/VFX/pose sprites, not true 8-direction character sheets.
- Enemies & Bosses: useful mob vocabulary; direction/animation coverage still needs manual inspection.

**Not direct runtime art yet**

All downloaded FreePixel packs remain reference/intake material. Do not move raw assets into Unity runtime folders or train on them until license/training permission and art-direction approval are explicit.
